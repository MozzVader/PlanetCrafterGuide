ABOUT THE GAME

What is Planet Crafter?

🌍

Terraform

You're a convict abandoned on a barren planet. Your mission is to transform this desolate world into a habitable planet for humanity. Increase oxygen, heat, pressure, and biomass until you reach a Terraformation Index of 25 million.

⛏️

Explore & Gather

Travel across the map collecting essential minerals like iron, cobalt, titanium, uranium, and the rare iridium and osmium. Find golden chests with seeds, blueprints, and valuable resources hidden in caves and ruins.

🔧

Build

Craft terraforming machines, ore extractors, solar panels, rockets, and more. Each new machine unlocks new crafting possibilities and brings you one step closer to your final goal.

🧬

Create Life

From the first algae to insects, animals, and birds, watch as your planet gradually comes alive. Each terraformation stage brings new life forms and transforms the landscape before your eyes.

The Terraformation Index (Ti)

Your planet's progress is measured by the Terraformation Index, which is the sum of four key categories: Oxygen, Heat, Pressure, and Biomass. Each category has its own machines and strategies. You don't need to balance them perfectly, but advancing in all simultaneously unlocks technologies faster and gets you closer to the goal of 25,000,000 Ti.

BEGINNING

Getting Started

Everything you need to know to survive the first days and lay the foundations of your base.

1

Day 0: Arrival & Survival

You are a convict who struck a deal: terraform this planet or die alone. When choosing difficulty, pick "standard" or "relaxing" and "standard start" location — it's centered between all the main resources. You land in a dried-up lake bed with a pod, a basic crafting station, and a small chest. Your mission is to raise the Terraformation Index (T.I.) high enough for the planet to become habitable.

You have three survival bars: Hunger (recharge by eating, decays slowly), Thirst (recharge with bottled water, decays quite fast), and Oxygen (only drops outdoors; recharges inside sealed rooms or with O2 canisters). The only things that can hurt you are falls and meteor storms. There are no enemies or alien beasts — you're completely on your own.

Important: The lake bed where you landed will flood when your T.I. reaches the rain and lakes stages. Don't build your permanent base there.

💡 Always carry at least 1 food, 2 O2 canisters, and 2 water bottles when leaving base. O2 canisters and water bottles recharge the bar FULLY — don't waste them when you're still at half.
2

Day 1: Basic Tools

The first things you need to craft at your pod's station are the essential tools. Build the Construction Chip (2 Silicon + 2 Magnesium), then the Deconstruction Chip (1 Silicon + 1 Magnesium) and the Flashlight (1 Silicon + 2 Magnesium). Total: 4 Silicon + 5 Magnesium.

You also need survival supplies: 2 ice for 2 water bottles and 4 cobalt for 2 O2 canisters. Then upgrade your gear with a T1 O2 Tank and T1 Backpack (together they require 3 Iron, 2 Cobalt, 1 Magnesium). Equip both in your "gear" slots — you only have 4 slots, so stash the flashlight in the chest for now.

Minerals on the ground never respawn after you collect them. Only meteorites and Ore Extractors generate more. You don't need to grab EVERYTHING — plan what you need to craft first.

💡 Learn to recognize minerals: Cobalt = big blue crystal, Magnesium = cluster of black crystals, Iron = grey/black shiny lump, Titanium = tan lumpy sphere (only half visible), Silicon = black lump like melted plastic.
3

Day 1 (cont.): Your First Base on the Hill

In front of your pod there's a hill with a flat plain on top. That's the ideal spot for your first base: high enough to avoid flooding and centrally located between mineral deposits. Before heading up, carry enough materials to build immediately upon arrival:

Living Compartment + Door: 3 Iron, 2 Titanium, 1 Silicon
Crafting Station T2: 1 Iron + 1 Silicon
2 extra water bottles: 2 Ice
2 extra O2 canisters: 4 Cobalt
2 storage chests: 2 Iron

Build your room on the flat part of the hilltop, NOT on the slope or in the lake bed. Add a door and a T2 crafting station inside. This new station unlocks the Exoskeleton (1 Titanium + 1 Silicon + 1 Magnesium) which gives you extra slots on your toolbelt.

💡 When building a second room, look for the chain icon — it indicates rooms will connect with a hallway. Without it, they'll be separate blocks and you'll need a door to enter.
4

Day 2: Power & Terraforming Machines

The crafting station is the only thing that works without external power. For everything else, you need power. The good news is that in Planet Crafter, power is wireless — it covers the entire planet, so you can build generators wherever you want. Start with 8 Wind Turbines (1 Iron each). They're cheap but inefficient; you'll unlock better solar panels and nuclear reactors later.

At your base, build all three computer terminals: the power monitor (to track consumption), the blueprints monitor (where you trade Data Chips for secret blueprints), and the terraforming screen (to see your T.I., temperature, pressure, and oxygen progress).

Start with terraforming machines: Drills (1 Titanium + 1 Iron) raise atmospheric pressure. T1 Vegetubes (1 Iron + 1 Ice + 1 Magnesium each, go indoors) raise oxygen — they need flower seeds installed, and each type gives a different bonus. T1 Heaters raise temperature but need Iridium, which you don't have yet. Build at least 5 drills to unlock upgrades.

Your main goal now: reach 12.00 ppt of Oxygen to unlock the Food Grower blueprint before you run out of space food.

💡 T1 heaters go INSIDE sealed rooms, not outside. Drills go outside on flat ground. T1 vegetubes go inside. Read each building's description in the construction menu.
5

Day 3: First Shipwreck

Behind your base there's a small shipwreck against a cliff face. It's time to explore it — we need more flower seeds for the vegetubes to boost oxygen faster. But before you approach, prepare properly.

Golden rule of shipwrecks: items lying on the ground (not in chests) vanish in less than 20 minutes after you get close. The timer does NOT start until you approach. You need a big backpack, flashlight (it's dark inside), and enough O2 canisters.

Build an outpost near the shipwreck entrance: room with door, chests, T2 crafting station, 2 turbines, and a lamp. Go in with your backpack as empty as possible and grab everything. If you find Data Chips, bring them to the blueprints monitor at your base to trade for secret blueprints.

On your return, install all flower seeds into vegetubes and build 2 Food Growers with vegetable seeds. Prioritize eating beans — they restore the most health and aren't used in research later. Save squash, eggplant, and mushrooms for the biolab.

💡 Always empty chests completely and then deconstruct them. You get back 1 Iron (blue chest) or 1 Super Alloy + 1 Aluminum (gold chest), and you avoid confusion later thinking they still have something inside.
6

Day 4-5: Iridium, Aluminum & Heaters

With T2 tank and backpack you can walk farther. There are two key locations: the Iridium Cave (follow the rocky cliff where reddish sand falls; iridium is RED crystals) and the Aluminum Field (grey sand with spiky silver rocks, on the other side of the lake bed). Both will be your T2 mineral source for the rest of the game.

Collect everything and build T1 Heaters inside a room — they raise temperature as soon as you build them. Replace your T1 drills and vegetubes with T2/T3 versions that are much more efficient but consume more power. Add solar panels to keep your grid stable.

Also build an Atmospheric Water Collector (generates water passively without ice) and make sure you have at least 4 Food Growers running with different vegetable seeds.

💡 Chests give better loot the higher your T.I. has progressed. If you spot chests far from your base, note their location and come back later when your T.I. is higher — they'll have rarer and more valuable items.
7

Day 5-7: Uranium, Rockets & Ore Extractors

To keep progressing you need uranium (for rocket engines and nuclear reactors) and Ore Extractors (for automated mining). The uranium cave is quite far — you'll need to build 2-3 outposts along the way, each with a room, crafting station, storage, bean food grower, and water collector.

The uranium cave has a greenish glow when you get close. Build a permanent mini-base there with an Advanced Crafting Station to convert uranium into rods and save backpack space.

Back at your base, build a Rocket Platform (3 Super Alloy + 3 Iron + 3 Titanium). The recommended launch order is:

1. T1 GPS Satellite — activates map screens
2. T2 GPS Satellite — improves map resolution
3-4. Asteroid Attraction Rockets — bring uranium and boost temperature
5-7. Magnetic Field Protection Rockets — bring iridium and boost pressure

Never use your last 4 pieces of uranium — save them for an emergency asteroid rocket. Without uranium you can't launch rockets, and it could take weeks until the game randomly sends a uranium meteor.

Build T1 Ore Extractors at the iridium cave (red sand) and the aluminum field (grey sand). On regular terrain they extract iron, titanium, silicon, magnesium, and cobalt.

💡 You can demolish any building and get back 100% of the materials. And with the Recycler you can convert manufactured items (rocket engines, uranium rods, super alloy, O2 canisters) back into raw materials. Nothing is truly lost.
8

Day 8+: Mid-Game

Congratulations, you survived the early game. From here the game opens up: build T2 Ore Extractors to mine osmium, sulfur, uranium, and super alloy. Explore The Labyrinth (packed with super alloy, aluminum, and golden chests). Set up your Biolab with biodomes and algae generators. Craft bioplastic, bacteria, and fertilizer. Launch spore rockets to find special seeds. And discover the planet's history through emails, ship logs, and journals found in shipwrecks and underground vaults.

Your main bottlenecks from now on will be your supply of Osmium, Iridium, Super Alloy, and Uranium. Balance your power grid, expand your mines, and above all... launch more rockets!

💡 Use Beacons to mark important locations. You can change their color and name, and the distance will show on your compass. They're cheap (1 Ti, 1 Si, 1 Al) and save you a ton of headaches.

PROGRESSION

Terraformation Stages

The 15 stages that transform a sterile desert into a planet full of life.

1

Barren

Ti 0

The planet as you find it. No atmosphere, no water, no life. Just you and your tools.

2

Blue Sky

Ti 175 kTi

The sky changes color and turns blue. Your first real victory!

3

Clouds

Ti 350 kTi

Clouds start forming in the sky. The planet looks more alive than ever.

4

Rain

Ti 875 kTi

Rain appears for the first time. Water begins falling from the sky onto the barren terrain.

5

Liquid Water

Ti 3 MTi

Liquid water accumulates on the surface. Rivers and streams begin to form.

6

Lakes

Ti 50 MTi

Lakes form across the planet. Aquatic ecosystems begin to develop.

7

Moss

Ti 200 MTi

Green moss covers rocks and soil. The first plant life forms appear.

8

Flora

Ti 700 MTi

Vegetation explodes. Plants and flowers cover large areas of the planet.

9

Trees

Ti 2 GTi

Trees grow naturally all over the planet. The landscape transforms completely. Wow moment!

10

Insects

Ti 8 GTi

Insects appear and flowers bloom. The planet starts to have visible life. Build the DNA Manipulator!

11

Breathable Atmosphere

Ti 32 GTi

The atmosphere becomes breathable. You can take off your helmet now! (well, in the lore).

12

Fish

Ti 120 GTi

Fish swim in the lakes and rivers. Aquatic ecosystems are complete.

13

Amphibians

Ti 425 GTi

Amphibians inhabit the space between land and water. New life appears on your planet.

14

Mammals

Ti 1.25 TTi

Mammals start roaming your world. Complex and complete ecosystems.

15

Complete Terraformation

Ti 4 TTi

You did it! The planet is fully terraformed and ready for humanity. Congratulations, convict!

Location: Surface, crates, ore extractor

Light metallic mineral. Gateway to T3 machines. Find it in the Aluminum Hills and some caves.

Location: Crates, Aluminum Hills, some caves, ore extractor
💜

Iridium

Uncommon

Shiny violet mineral. Essential for T4 machines. Look in the Iridium Mine, meteorites, and advanced extractors.

Location: Crates, Iridium Mine, ore extractor, meteor events
☢️

Uranium

Uncommon

Shiny green mineral. Used in Thermal Plants, DNA Manipulators, and Rockets. Brought by meteorites.

Location: Crates, caves, meteor events, T2 ore extractor
🟠

Sulfur

Rare

Yellow crystals. Main fuel for T2 Burning Generators for heat. Look in the Sulfur Fields.

Location: Caves, Sulfur Fields, T3 ore extractor
⚙️

Super Alloy

Rare

Advanced component key for T3 machines. Can be crafted, found in crates, or extracted with advanced machines.

Location: Crates, Super Alloy Cave, T2 ore extractor, meteor events, crafting
🌈

Osmium

Very Rare

One of the rarest minerals in the game. Needed for everything T4. Find it in the Osmium Cave and meteorites.

Location: Osmium cave, T2 ore extractor, meteor events
🌿

Zeolite

Very Rare

Organic mineral that emerges as flora grows. Roots lift it from the ground starting at ~150 MTi, collectible from ~500 MTi. Fully available when reaching the Flora stage (700 MTi).

Location: Zeolite Cave, T2 ore extractor
🖤

Obsidian

Very Rare

Extremely hard volcanic rock. Only found in the Volcano Biome and with advanced extractors. Dangerous area!

Location: Volcano Biome, T2 ore extractor

PRO TIPS

Advanced Tips

Everything you wish you knew before starting (and some game-changing tricks).

01

Power is wireless

There are no power cables in Planet Crafter. Your electrical grid covers the entire planet — you can build generators anywhere and they'll power all your machines regardless of distance. Just be careful not to exceed your total production, because if you run out of power all machines stop instantly.

02

Demolish = full refund

You can demolish any building and get back 100% of the materials invested. Plus, the Recycler lets you convert manufactured items (rocket engines, uranium rods, super alloy, O2 canisters) back into raw materials. Nothing is truly lost, so don't be afraid to experiment.

03

Chests improve with your T.I.

Both blue and gold chests give better loot the higher your Terraformation Index has progressed. If you spot chests far from your base early on, note their location and come back later when your T.I. is higher — they'll have rarer and more valuable items.

04

The 20-minute shipwreck rule

Items lying on the ground inside a shipwreck (not in chests) vanish in less than 20 minutes after you get close. The timer does NOT start until you approach. Never enter a shipwreck without a big backpack, flashlight, and enough supplies to clear it all out.

05

Ground minerals don't respawn

Minerals sticking out of the ground are finite — once you collect them, they never come back. The only ways to get more are meteorites and Ore Extractors. Plan your exploration routes and don't waste trips.

06

Always save emergency uranium

Never use your last 4 pieces of uranium. Without uranium you can't build rocket engines, and it could take weeks until the game sends a uranium meteor. Always keep a small stash next to your Advanced Crafting Station and replace it immediately after use.

07

Eat beans, save the rest

Beans restore the most health and aren't used in any research recipe later on. Squash, eggplant, and mushrooms ARE needed for the biolab. Prioritize eating beans and save the other veggies for science.

08

Read seed descriptions carefully

Each type of flower seed gives a different oxygen bonus in vegetubes. Some give +25%, others give +500%. Before installing a seed, read its description so you don't waste the most valuable ones on T1 vegetubes you'll replace later.

EXPLORATION

Golden Chests & Secrets

Hidden treasure scattered across the planet. These chests contain items that can change your run.

📦

Golden Chests

There are over 20 golden chests hidden throughout the map. Each one contains unique items that can include: rare seeds (essential for biomass), advanced machine blueprints, and rare resources like iridium. Find them in caves, near wrecks, on mountaintops, and in unexpected places.

🗺️

Key Coordinates

Some important locations on the map worth exploring:

  • Icespires: Area with abundant ice. Approx coords: -1045, 165, -270
  • Lava Biome: Dangerous zone with rare minerals
  • The Arches: Spectacular rock formations. Approx coords: 10, 147, 1930
  • Iridium Caves: Best sources of iridium and osmium
  • Selenea (Moon): Special location unlockable in advanced stages
☄️

Meteorites

Every meteor shower leaves craters with valuable minerals. Visit the craters after each storm to collect iridium and other rare resources. As the game progresses, storms bring more valuable meteorites. Don't waste this free resource source!

🏗️

Wrecks

Find remains of previous ships with golden chests and resources. They're like mini-dungeons to explore. Each wreck has its own environment and challenges. Use beacons to mark them and return later if you don't have the right equipment.
